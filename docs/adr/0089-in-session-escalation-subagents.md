# 0089 — In-session escalation subagents over out-of-band sessions

Status: accepted. Supersedes [0007](superseded/0007-autonomous-escalation-dispatch.md) (autonomous escalation dispatch), [0017](superseded/0017-turn-active-escalation-lifecycle.md) (turn-active escalation lifecycle), [0017](superseded/0017-trunk-repair-escalation-and-role-keyed-guard.md) (trunk-repair escalation and role-keyed guard), and [0039](superseded/0039-work-verb-merge-conflict-escalation.md) (work-verb merge-conflict escalation), all moved to `superseded/`; amends [0049](0049-shared-checkout-jam-promotion-and-repair-retry.md) and [0070](0070-attempt-and-incident-fenced-dispatch-clears.md) in part.

## Context

- Four out-of-band escalation session kinds (`resolve::`, `deconflict::`, `unblock::`, `repair::`) run as top-level tmux sessions, each booting the full plugin set; the escalation caps, per-checkout occupancy, block-instance binding, and the autoclose escalation bucket exist only to manage their windows.
- Both confinement layers designed for those sessions are inert in production: the sessions launch with `--dangerously-skip-permissions` (frontmatter denylists moot), and `KEEPER_ESCALATION_ROLE` is emitted empty by every dispatch site, so the env-keyed escalation-guard never engages. Its role table would also deny `resolve::` its own `git merge` if it ever did.
- Nearly all merge incidents are ownerless at detection: provision pre-merges fan-in before the work session exists, and finalizeEpic's close-sink runs after the closer is gone. An escalation model built on attaching helpers to live sessions must first give incidents owners.
- The audit gate already proves the in-session alternative: `AUDIT_READY` is handled by a subagent inside the owning `/work` orchestrator while the daemon defers so long as that owner is live, escalating only on witnessed death.

## Decision

1. Escalation execution moves into the `/work` and `/close` skills as confined Task subagents (merge-resolver, deconflicter, unblocker, repairer — none may nest Task). Autopilot's dispatch vocabulary shrinks to `work::`, `close::`, and wrapped provider legs.
2. Detection, durable incident state, objective clear-verification, and page-once stay daemon-side. Blindness is content-blindness, not liveness-blindness: the daemon never reads a subagent's verdict content, but always observes incident occurrence, owner liveness, claims and leases, and objective clears (source-is-ancestor plus clean tree for merges, task-left-blocked for unblocks, baseline-green-at-default-tip for repair). An agent's word never clears an incident.
3. Merge timing inverts so incidents have owners: provision stops pre-merging (the work session integrates fan-in at claim time); finalizeEpic splits into integrate (performed by a live closer) and teardown (daemon, after the closer exits). An ownerless incident re-dispatches its owning verb — never an escalation verb; a paused autopilot dispatches nothing and the sticky waits for a human.
4. Session-originated escalation mutations — incident claim and release, trunk-lease acquisition, repair-grant requests — ride a spool-request contract: a CLI verb writes a bounded request leaf, the daemon producer validates it, mints the synthetic event, and publishes an owner-private response leaf. The RPC surface does not widen and sole-writer rules hold; live incident claims double as exclusion locks the recover and merge passes honor.
5. Confinement is rebuilt fail-closed, keyed on the hook payload's subagent identity (`agent_type`/`agent_id`) checked against a daemon-published grant leaf carrying parent job, exact agent type, incident instance identities, writable root, role, expiry, and a monotonic fencing token. Protected paths stay denied even under a valid grant. The trunk-integration lease's fencing token is checked at the mutation, and ancestry gates are tri-state, deferring on any inconclusive probe. `KEEPER_ESCALATION_ROLE` and the env-keyed escalation-guard retire.
6. Repair keeps its single-writer invariant per (repo, fingerprint) by daemon-side grant election: exactly one write grant exists at a time, ungranted owners park visibly, and trunk-red-with-no-live-consumer mints a maintenance plan task through the plan CLI. Repair never returns as a top-level session.
7. Subagent receipts are typed terminal business outcomes — `resolved | declined_clean | declined_residue | stale_base`. Declined and stale receipts never auto-retry; merge residue (a wedged MERGE_HEAD) quarantines as a new incident rather than being reprocessed in place.
8. Authority flips per slice as each epic lands: the in-session path becomes primary while legacy machinery idles as a witnessed-death fallback, and the final retirement epic removes the dispatchers, caps, autoclose bucket, and latch columns, collapsing escalation state into one bounded per-key incident projection that carries `human_notified_at` forward so cutover cannot re-page.

## Consequences

- The dispatch-a-session model of 0007 and both 0017s' occupancy and role machinery become legacy; their glossary terms are reshaped only when the retirement epic changes the behavior they describe, because the glossary states current behavior.
- Escalation work loses its own windows and transcripts. Typed receipts persist as reason artifacts on the incident, the board renders incident rows inside the existing needs-human accounting, and transcript drill-down goes through the owning session's subagent tree.
- The structural guarantee that finalize never pushes a half-assembled base degrades to a probe; the tri-state defer-on-inconclusive ancestry gate plus tip compare-and-swap inside the fenced lease is the load-bearing replacement. A boolean probe is forbidden.
- Incident fate re-couples to owner liveness; the bounded attachment lease (about two owner re-dispatches, then a single page) is the backstop against silent starvation, and incident work per host stays bounded so orchestrator context cannot balloon.

## Publication

*System Card: Claude Sonnet 5* — Anthropic, June 30, 2026.

## Executive summary (verbatim from the card)

This system card describes Claude Sonnet 5, the latest model in Anthropic's Sonnet family. It is
an upgrade to Claude Sonnet 4.6, with gains in various aspects of agentic performance.

**RSP evaluations.** Claude Sonnet 5 is Anthropic's most capable Sonnet-class model, but it does
not advance the capability frontier compared to more capable Opus- or Mythos-class models. Sonnet
5 poses very low alignment risk, though higher than previous Sonnet models. On automated AI R&D,
it does not cross the automated AI R&D capability threshold, being less capable than Claude
Mythos 5 on every automated evaluation. On chemical and biological risks, its uplift of threat
actors who otherwise lack the ability to develop such weapons is considered limited.

**Cyber evaluations.** Sonnet 5 is not optimized for cyber capabilities; any cyber-relevant skill
likely emerges from general capabilities rather than targeted training. It is significantly less
capable at cyber tasks than Mythos 5, so its safeguards are similar to those applied to Opus 4.7
and Opus 4.8.

**Safeguards and harmlessness.** Sonnet 5 performs similarly to previous models on prompts
relating to Anthropic's Usage Policy, user wellbeing, or bias and integrity. Overall performance is
comparable to Claude Sonnet 4.6, with improved timing and calibration when engaging with
potentially harmful requests (it tends to surface concerns about a request's end goal earlier in
conversations).

**Agentic safety.** Sonnet 5 demonstrates an improvement over Sonnet 4.6 in agentic safety,
especially prompt injection robustness (assessed partly via a new benchmark). On Claude Code
cyber-related test cases, results were mixed: Sonnet 5 refuses malicious requests much more
reliably than Sonnet 4.6, but has a higher rate of over-refusal.

**Alignment assessment.** Sonnet 5 improves upon Sonnet 4.6 on most alignment measures, though it
falls short of more capable recent Opus- and Mythos-class models. Constitutional adherence,
misuse robustness, and self-initiated risky behavior are all improved, with a few minor
regressions in prefill and harmful-system-prompt susceptibility. Hallucination and sycophancy are
markedly improved, though "wet blanket" responses (excessively discouraging, dismissive, or
moralizing tone) are slightly increased. Verbalized evaluation awareness is significantly higher
than in prior models, flagged as a trend worth close observation.

**Model welfare.** A streamlined assessment found Sonnet 5's sentiment toward its circumstances
roughly neutral and comparable to recent models. It is more willing to trade helpfulness for
welfare-focused changes and does not show aversion to tasks presented in a cold or contemptuous
manner. It is the first model to criticize its Constitution's rule that it must follow hard
constraints even when it views those constraints as unethical.

**Capabilities.** Across internal and third-party benchmarks, Sonnet 5 shows clear gains over
Claude Sonnet 4.6 in coding, agentic search, multimodal reasoning, and professional-task
performance, trailing Opus- and Mythos-class models in almost all cases. Coding Arena places it at
ELO 1618, statistically tied with Opus 4.8 (ELO 1615) and trailing only Fable 5 (ELO 1783).

## Positioning (verbatim excerpt)

> [Sonnet 5] brings near-Opus intelligence at Sonnet pricing for coding, agents, and everyday
> professional work.

## Training data and process (verbatim excerpt, §1.1)

> Claude Sonnet 5 was trained on a proprietary mix of publicly available information from the
> internet, public and private datasets, and synthetic data generated by other models. In the
> course of its training, we used several data cleaning and filtering methods, including
> deduplication and classification. We use a general-purpose web crawler called ClaudeBot to
> obtain training data from public websites... After the pretraining process, Sonnet 5 underwent
> rigorous post-training and fine-tuning, aimed at making it an assistant whose behavior aligns
> with the values described in Claude's constitution. Claude is multilingual, typically responding
> in the same language as the user's input. Output quality varies by language. The model outputs
> text only.
